Have you ever seen videos of an AI trying to detect something that is completely obvious to us humans, but it fails miserably? Our AI’s objective is to detect if a plant is healthy or not. However, it made many errors, which made it extremely ineffective unless specific circumstances were met. Let’s get right into it.

In our training process, we only included individual leaves, or just a few of them. Because of this, our AI only works when the analyzed plant’s photo is taken from a close distance. This allows the program to effectively analyze whether individual leaves are healthy or not.
For our AI training, we used 20 pictures of healthy leaves and 20 pictures of unhealthy leaves. It’s worth noting that we gathered all the pictures from the internet, which, when compared to the pictures we took ourselves, were of a much higher quality.

However, one problem with this is that we didn’t include a category for things that are neither one nor the other. One consequence of this is that whenever the AI detects something that is not a leaf (like a human, for example), it automatically classifies it as an unhealthy plant, simply because most of these objects aren’t completely green.

This is similar to someone with myopia trying to read something far away without glasses. It’s not that they lack the intelligence to understand it; rather, the text is just illegible to them. The problem is not the text itself, but rather the person's vision.

Furthermore, this wasn’t even the biggest problem we encountered. To test if our AI worked, we took some photos around our school. However, we basically only took photos from a certain distance (from 2 to 4 meters), which heavily influenced the way the machine read the plants.

Since all of the photos used to train the AI were taken from really close up, it led to the AI confusing the actual healthy plants we photographed with unhealthy plants. The only solution we could find was to zoom in on our photos so that the program could see the individual leaves, or at least a few of them.
Ultimately, our AI still has a lot of room for improvement, such as recognizing when something is not a plant and better analyzing trees or potted plants.
