Do you think deciding if a posture is good or bad is easy? For our AI, it isn’t. It can’t be 100% sure until an extremely good or extremely bad posture is shown. Depending on what is shown to our AI, it can be difficult to analyze; however, for a human, it may not be that hard. 

We trained our Teachable Machine model on two main classes: “Good Posture” (which showed a person (Viveiros or Fon) sitting in a chair with good posture) and “Bad Posture” (which showed a person (Viveiros or Fon) sitting in a chair with bad posture). We gave it 100 images each to analyze and changed the epochs from 50 to 100. 

The difficulties that surprised us throughout the project led to a better understanding of how AI is better taught using an optimal method rather than just an okay method. 

Determining if a posture is good or bad for an AI is hard due to a few reasons: 

* **Uploaded Image Problem:** If an image is uploaded from Google or any other website, the AI may get confused because it cannot determine exactly the curvature of the person's back, which can result in the completely opposite result being determined by the AI.
* **Epochs 50:** By not changing the epochs to 100, the AI will only analyze/study the images a few times. This can lead to an imprecise answer or even the opposite answer. 

Teaching an AI can be hard, but knowing how to teach it makes the process easier. Understanding the best method to teach our AI made the process way easier than it was at first, when we were not using the optimal way of teaching it. Teaching AI can be a challenge; however, seeing the results and gaining a better understanding of how it works and how it learns makes understanding AI a true pleasure.
